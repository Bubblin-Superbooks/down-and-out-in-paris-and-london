He told me (one could see his French soul revelling in the pedantry of it) that I had not sufficient papers of identification; my carte d'identit� was not enough, and I must show a passport or addressed envelopes. Boris had addressed envelopes by the score, but his carte d'identit� was out of order (he never renewed it, so as to avoid the tax), so we could not pawn the overcoats in his name. All we could do was to trudge up to my room, get the necessary papers, and take the coats to the pawnshop in the Boulevard Port Royal.

I left Boris at my room and went down to the pawnshop. When I got there I found that it was shut and would not open till four in the afternoon. It was now about half-past one, and I had walked twelve kilometres and had no food for sixty hours. Fate seemed to be playing a series of extraordinarily unamusing jokes.
